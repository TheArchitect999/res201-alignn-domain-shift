# Combined Joint Paper Blueprint (v2)

Purpose: Structure the joint paper according to the project brief logic: oxide results, nitride results, direct comparison, and embedding analysis. The paper should treat oxide as the control, nitride as the out-of-distribution test, and the embedding section as the mechanism-oriented interpretation layer.

## Revision notes (v2)

- The old combined nitride subsection "Zero-shot penalty and inert low-N fine-tuning" has been split into two separate subsections: "Zero-shot domain-shift penalty" and "Inert low-N fine-tuning (N <= 200)". This makes Results I (oxide) and Results II (nitride) structurally parallel and prevents the ZS penalty from being absorbed into the inertness narrative.
- Results I, II, and III now all re-enter in the same three-dimensional order (ZS -> FT -> scratch), so Results III reads as a genuine side-by-side rather than a repackaging.
- Results IV retains its geometric-observation -> mechanism-test order, which is the correct shape for an embedding-analysis block.

## Paper-level guardrails

- Follow the brief-specified order: Introduction, Methods, Results I (oxide), Results II (nitride), Results III (direct comparison), Results IV (embedding analysis), Discussion, Conclusion, References.
- Keep nitride `N <= 200` framed as inert fine-tuning.
- Keep nitride `N=50` transfer benefit framed as initialization advantage, not adaptation gain.
- Use `last_alignn_pool` as the main embedding layer in text and figures.
- Do not imply from-scratch comparisons exist at `N=10`, `100`, `200`, or `1000`.
