## Why it was created

The Stage 5 literature and introduction planning pack is approved for intro drafting. Three active authority files govern all future drafting:

- `literature_claim_map_v3.md`
- `introduction_paragraph_plan_v4.md`
- `citation_needed_list_v2.md`

Without a single point-of-entry document, future drafting agents — human or AI — risk:
- opening a superseded Stage 5 variant and following its stale JARVIS hierarchy;
- defaulting to Hu et al. 2024 as the lead transfer-learning citation;
- using JARVIS 2025 or JARVIS-Leaderboard 2024 as generic JARVIS anchors instead of JARVIS 2020 for provenance sentences;
- writing an overbroad gap claim;
- writing result-forward language in the introduction.

`STAGE5_DRAFTING_HANDOFF.md` closes those regression paths by providing a single compact document that names the active authority files, states the non-negotiable rules, restates the citation hierarchies, enforces sentence-type discipline, and provides a pre-flight checklist and a reusable agent preamble block.

## What this is

This is a workflow-control layer for future drafting phases. It is not a literature review, not a methods document, and not a revision of any scientific planning artifact. Its only function is to ensure that future drafting sessions — regardless of which agent or person performs them — begin from the same authority base and apply the same hard rules that the Stage 5 consistency pack established.
